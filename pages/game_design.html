ASTRO BLAST

ABOUT THE PROJECT

For a school project, I developed 'Astro Blast,' a 2D retro platformer. This game was created from start to finish within an 8-week timeframe. Players navigate through levels, fighting enemies using a sword and a gun. A key feature is the choice of a special power at the start of each level—'Super Speed' or 'Time Freeze'—which provides a unique way to play. The objective is to defeat the level's boss and reach the final checkpoint to win.

PLAYER

The player character has several actions, including a double jump for extra mobility. All character animations are rendered from spritesheets. The player has a hp system of 100HP and will take damage when touching an enemy.

ABILITIES

At the start of each level, players can choose between three unique powers:

SUPER SPEED

Dash in super speed with extra velocity.

TIME FREEZE

Slow down time for enemies temporarily.

SUPER SAIYAN

A power boost to your attacks. This didn't make it into the game on time.

All these powers were intended for a class based system which unfortunately didn't make it in. The time-freeze power was inspired from Dante's Quicksilver in the Devil May Cry series.

ENEMIES

The enemies are inspired by classic platformers like Super Mario. Just touching one will cause the player to take damage. Each one has a unique movement pattern that you'll need to learn to avoid them. They can be defeated with either melee attacks or by shooting them.

EAGLE

A flying sentinel that dives to attack.

SLIME

A basic ground unit that slowly moves towards the player.

CYBORG

A fast-moving cyborg that charges at the player.

BOSS

At the end of each level, players will face a final boss. This enemy has increased health and uses two attack patterns: a direct melee attack if the player is nearby and a sudden teleport to get close to the player. Defeating this boss will complete the level.

MELEE ATTACK

The boss runs towards the player and performs a melee swipe attack.

TELEPORT

The boss can also teleport next to the player to perform a melee attack.

PARALLAX BACKGROUND

As for the background of my game, I found a static background too bland. This is when I searched and found a technique called parallax scrolling. To implement this, I divided a static background image into several parts and made them move at different speeds. The furthest layer moves the slowest, while the closest layer moves the fastest as demonstrated below:
